How to Make Jag (Cape Verde Rice)

This Cape Verde side called Jagacida is just known as "Jag". There are numerous forms of this dish so you can get as inventive as you would like. Depending on the number of things you that add, it could undoubtedly be a great lunch also. I love the Linguica sausage. Serve this Cape Verde jagacida with some octopus stew and a basic green plate of mixed greens for a true and delectable Cape Verde dinner.

Did you know that Cape Verde is also called Cabo Verde and is an island off the coast of West Africa? When the island was discovered and colonized by the Portuguese in the 15th century, it was uninhabited. The island became a trading center for African slaves which was abolished in 1876. Today it has one of the most stable governments in Africa.

Ingredients

  • 1 ¼ cup long grain white rice
  • 2 ½ cups water
  • 1 large bay leaf
  • 1 lb. Linguica sausage or turkey sausage equivalent
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 cup frozen lima beans
  • 1 medium onion chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic minced
  • ¼ teaspoon white pepper
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil.

How to make

  1. Heat the oil in a sauce pan and fry the sausage, season with salt and pepper
  2. Add the onions and garlic stir until onions are tender but taking care not to burn garlic
  3. Add water, tomato paste
  4. Add in the lima beans and rice stirring to combine, bring to a boil
  5. Turn down to simmer and cover. Cook 20-30 minutes until liquid is absorbed and rice is tender.
  6. Remove the bay leaf and serve.
